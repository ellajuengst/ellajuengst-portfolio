designing my website and brand

motivation and criteria

I decided to make this website to rebrand my portfolio and apply my computer science knowledge to create a new platform to share my work. I began by brainstorming the style I wanted my work, website, and brand to represent. I settled on the keywords: clean, consistent, friendly, and charming. I aimed to base my design around these for words to create an aesthetic that represents my brand.

design palettes

Keeping my keywords in mind, I began to explore options for my typography. I chose objekiv mk2 because of its clean, modern, sans sarif face. I then found Goudy Book Letter 1911, which I think perfectly compliments objekiv mk2 as a sarif font for my other text.

I had previously decided on an “arctic wildlife” theme for my illustrations. When choosing my color palette, I knew I wanted to include blues to incorporate the theme of ice and water. I chose a dark blue for my text and white for the background of my site. The two medium shades of blue serve in my illustrations. I chose a peachy orange color as my accent color for buttons and the animal illustrations and a darker version for hover capabilities.

logo design

I used my fonts as a starting point for designing my logo. I wanted the logo to involve my initials. I began by creating the first logo shown. I liked the way the letters formed a square, but later in the design process, I decided to make all the typography on my website lowercase to create a more relaxed feel. Therefore, I determined the uppercase initials would not work well with the rest of my design. I switched the initials to lower case and added an iceberg in between. The iceberg relates my logo to the rest of the illustrations on my site, and also abstractly represents my middle initial, "A."

ui elements

The UI elements of my page are simple and clean (again going back to my keywords). I gave the buttons the orange accent color and the navigation controls the same dark blue as the text.

illustrations

To create my illustrations, I first studied references of icebergs, arctic hares, and polar bears, and I practiced drawing them in my sketchbook. Once I had ideas nailed down, I planned out what type of illustrations I wanted for each webpage. I created the illustrations as vector drawings in Adobe Illustrator, utilizing my website’s color palette to create consistency.
